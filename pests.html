Pest field guide

Know what you're dealing with.

Half of pest control is naming the thing. Below are the pests we treat most, in plain English — the signs you'll actually notice, why each one matters, and the plan that keeps it outside your perimeter.

Spot something that matches? You don't have to diagnose it perfectly. Tell us what you're seeing and we'll confirm it on the first visit.

🐜

Ants

Spring–Summer
Signs

Trails along baseboards and counters, little dirt piles by cracks, a sudden line straight to the pet bowl.

Why it matters

A few scouts mean a colony nearby. Sprays kill what you see and scatter the rest — the nest just keeps producing.

Handled by Home Protection
🪵

Termites

Spring swarms
Signs

Pencil-width mud tubes on the foundation, wood that sounds hollow, discarded wings on the sill after a warm day.

Why it matters

They cause billions in home damage a year and rarely show themselves until it's structural. With termites, speed is everything.

Handled by Termite Defense
🦟

Mosquitoes & ticks

Late spring–Fall
Signs

Bites after dusk in the yard, standing water in gutters and saucers, ticks on pets after time in tall grass.

Why it matters

Both can carry disease, and they breed fast in the warm, wet spots you walk past every day without noticing.

Handled by Yard Enjoyment
🐭

Rodents

Fall–Winter
Signs

Rice-sized droppings under the sink, gnaw marks on packaging, scratching in the walls or attic at night.

Why it matters

Mice chew wiring — a real fire risk — and contaminate food. In cold months, one pair becomes a nest fast.

Handled by Home Protection
🪳

Cockroaches

Year-round
Signs

A musty smell, pepper-like specks in cabinets, and the classic: one darting for cover when you flip the light.

Why it matters

Roaches spread bacteria and trigger asthma and allergies. If you've seen one, more are hidden nearby.

Handled by Home Protection
🕷️

Spiders

Fall, indoors
Signs

Webs in corners, garages, and window frames; papery egg sacs; more sightings as the weather cools outside.

Why it matters

Most are harmless, but a few local species aren't — and webs mean other insects are getting in for them to eat.

Handled by Home Protection
🐝

Wasps & hornets

Summer
Signs

Papery nests under eaves and decks, steady traffic to one spot, and a defensive mood when you get close.

Why it matters

Stings hurt and can be dangerous if you're allergic. A big nest by a doorway is a hazard — not a DIY job.

Handled by Home Protection
🛡️

Stink bugs

Fall
Signs

Shield-shaped bugs sunning on walls and windows, then more of them indoors as they hunt for a warm winter spot.

Why it matters

Harmless, but relentless once inside — and squashing them earns the name. Sealing the perimeter keeps them out.

Handled by Home Protection
