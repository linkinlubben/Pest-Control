What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files a site stores on your device to make it work, remember your choices, and understand how it's used. Similar technologies include local storage, pixels, and tags. Cookies can be "session" (deleted when you close your browser) or "persistent" (they stay until they expire or you remove them).

Types of cookies we use

  • Strictly necessary — required for the site to function, such as remembering your place in the booking steps. These can't be switched off.
  • Preferences — remember choices like your region or that you've dismissed a banner, to improve your experience.
  • Analytics — help us understand which pages are visited and how the site performs, so we can improve it. These are only set if you've added an analytics tool (e.g. Google Analytics).
  • Marketing — used to measure campaigns or show relevant ads, typically set by third parties. Only present if you've added such tools.

As shipped, this template sets only strictly-necessary functionality and no third-party tracking. Update this list when you add tools.

Third-party cookies

Some cookies may be set by third parties whose services we use — for example an analytics provider, an embedded map, or a scheduling widget. Those providers control their own cookies under their own policies. Review each provider's policy for details.

Managing cookies

You can control and delete cookies through your browser settings — most browsers let you block or remove them, and warn you before accepting new ones. Blocking strictly-necessary cookies may break parts of the site.

  • Browser help pages for Chrome, Safari, Firefox, and Edge each explain how to manage cookies.
  • For analytics, you can often opt out through the provider (for example, Google Analytics offers a browser opt-out add-on).
  • If you add a consent banner, visitors can change their choices there at any time.

Do Not Track

Some browsers offer a "Do Not Track" signal. There is no common industry standard for responding to it, so our site currently does not respond to DNT signals. We'll update this notice if that changes.

Changes to this notice
